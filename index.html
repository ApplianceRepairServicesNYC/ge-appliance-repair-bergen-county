Common GE Washer Issues and Solutions

GE washers are built tough, but everyday use in Bergen County homes can lead to wear. Here's a deep dive into frequent problems and how we resolve them:

Noisy Operation

Rattling or thumping during spins often signals unbalanced loads, worn tub bearings, or loose drive belts. In older models, this might stem from motor coupler failure. Our process involves a full inspection: leveling the machine, tightening components, and replacing bearings if needed. For instance, in the GE GTW335ASNWW model, we've seen bearing issues after 5-7 years; a timely replacement can add years to its life.

Water Leaks

Leaks are a nightmare, potentially damaging floors or basements. Causes include cracked hoses, faulty water pumps, or deteriorated door boots in front-loaders. We use pressure testing to identify leaks, then seal or replace parts. Pro tip: Check hoses annually, especially in colder Bergen winters where freezing can cause bursts.

Failure to Start or Power On

This could be a tripped circuit, faulty power cord, or a malfunctioning control boardcommon in smart models with electronic glitches. We start with basic troubleshooting (resetting the breaker), then delve into board repairs or replacements, often resolving issues in under an hour.

Incomplete or Erratic Cycles

Sensors detecting load size or water temperature can fail, causing cycles to skip or repeat. Valves, thermistors, or timers are usual culprits. Our technicians recalibrate sensors and test cycles post-repair to ensure consistency.

Error Codes and Diagnostics

GE washers display codes like "dE" (door error) or "oE" (overflow). We decode these using GE's error library, addressing root causes like pressure switch failures. For Wi-Fi-enabled models, we also troubleshoot connectivity issues via the GE Appliances app.

Drainage Problems

Slow draining or standing water often results from clogged filters or pumps, exacerbated by lint or foreign objects. We clean filters (located at the front or back) and inspect hoses, recommending regular maintenance to avoid backups.

Spin Cycle Failures

If clothes come out sopping wet, it might be a drive motor issue or lid switch problem in top-loaders. We test RPMs and replace motors, ensuring balanced spins.

By addressing these proactively, we prevent secondary damage, like mold growth in damp interiors.
